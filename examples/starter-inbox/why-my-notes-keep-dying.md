# why my notes keep dying (venting, but there's a pattern)

count of note systems i've abandoned: paper notebooks, evernote, a wiki on a
home server, apple notes, obsidian (twice). every single one died the same
way, and it wasn't at the capture end — capturing is easy for the first month.

they died at the *organizing* end. the moment a system needed me to decide
where a note goes, or to go back and merge duplicates, or to keep the folder
structure honest, i had maybe three weeks of discipline in me. then folders
fork ("Meetings" vs "meeting-notes" vs "mtg" — i had all three), duplicates
pile up, and eventually i stop trusting my own system enough to search it.
once i stop searching it, it's dead, even though i kept adding to it for
months after.

so the requirement for anything new: i will only do capture and judgment.
organizing, linking, deduping, and telling me what's rotten has to be someone
else's job. previously "someone else" didn't exist. maybe now it does.
