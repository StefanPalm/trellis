# Weekly lint cadence

A single scheduled lint pass per week — Monday morning, in the first-week
retro — was enough to catch a forming `capturing` topic before it forked the
taxonomy (see [taxonomy drift](/failure-modes/taxonomy-drift.md)). The claim,
so far from one week of evidence: weekly is the *minimum* cadence that keeps
the wiki trustworthy, because trust is what keeps the wiki read, and an
unread wiki is a [junk drawer](/failure-modes/junk-drawer-wiki.md).

Two details that mattered:

- **Scheduled, not remembered.** Nothing visibly breaks when lint is skipped,
  so willpower-based linting decays exactly like every abandoned note system.
- **The report is a reading list, not a grade.** The retro's strongest
  observation was that the system "talks back": lint's open questions direct
  what to [capture](/practices/inbox-capture.md) next.

Unknown: whether high-volume weeks need a faster cadence, and whether lint
frequency interacts with [ingest batch size](/practices/ingest-batch-size.md).
