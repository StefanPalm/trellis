# Inbox capture

Capture is the entry point of the
[llm-wiki pattern](/foundations/llm-wiki-pattern.md): raw material goes into
the inbox unformatted and unsorted, and the agent files it at ingest time.
The division of labor is deliberate — deciding *where* a note belongs is
structure work, and structure work at capture time is friction that kills the
habit.

Two claims, each now independently supported:

- **Capture must cost under ~30 seconds.** In the first-week retro, the two
  days spent "cleaning up" notes before dropping them produced less than half
  the captured volume. Drag file, done, walk away.
- **Organizing the inbox is wasted work.** The agent re-derives structure at
  [ingest](/practices/ingest-batch-size.md) anyway; pre-sorting duplicates
  that effort and adds the friction above.

Cheap capture only pays off if ingest and lint actually run — an inbox that
only fills is the first step toward a
[junk-drawer wiki](/failure-modes/junk-drawer-wiki.md).
