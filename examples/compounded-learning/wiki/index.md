# compounded-learning — index

**Purpose:** answer, for a practitioner building the habit, which
compounded-learning practices actually work, why they work, and what evidence
supports them.

**Start here.** This file maps the bundle. The topic charter with rationales
is [topics.md](/topics.md); the change record is [log.md](/log.md); lint
reports live under [reports/](/reports/).

## practices

- [Inbox capture](/practices/inbox-capture.md) — Frictionless, unsorted
  capture into the inbox is the habit the whole loop depends on.
- [Ingest batch size](/practices/ingest-batch-size.md) — Whether to ingest
  items immediately or in related batches is contested; both positions and
  their evidence are stated.
- [Weekly lint cadence](/practices/weekly-lint-cadence.md) — One scheduled
  lint pass per week appears to be the minimum that preserves trust in the
  wiki.

## failure-modes

- [Junk-drawer wiki](/failure-modes/junk-drawer-wiki.md) — Ingesting without
  linting erodes trust until the wiki stops being read, which kills it.
- [Taxonomy drift](/failure-modes/taxonomy-drift.md) — Near-duplicate topics
  under different names silently fork the structure; naming discipline and
  lint are the countermeasures.

## foundations

- [The llm-wiki pattern](/foundations/llm-wiki-pattern.md) — Karpathy's
  sketch: an agent-maintained wiki as persistent memory, with judgment kept
  human and structure delegated.
- [Open Knowledge Format](/foundations/open-knowledge-format.md) — The
  markdown bundle format that makes each wiki portable beyond the agent that
  wrote it.
