# first week retro (raw)

what actually happened in week one of running the loop, while it's fresh:

capture: the under-30-seconds thing is real. the two days i tried to "clean
up" notes before dropping them in the inbox, i captured less than half as
much. drag file, done, walk away. confirmed for me.

ingest: here's the surprise. i ingested item-by-item the moment things landed
(as planned) and got two pages that were basically the same concept written
twice from two related articles processed a day apart. when i instead let
wednesday–friday pile up and ingested as one batch, the related stuff got
synthesized into one decent page. so: batching seems to produce *better*
pages, directly against my "always ingest immediately" instinct. tradeoff is
the wiki lags a few days. don't know where the line is.

lint: ran it once, scheduled, monday morning. it caught that i was about to
grow a `capturing` topic next to `practices` pages about capture — exactly
the naming fork i was worried about. one weekly pass feels like the minimum
that keeps trust; a wiki i don't trust is a wiki i stop reading, and then
it's just a junk drawer with frontmatter.

overall: the thing that separates this from every note system i've abandoned
is that the system talks back — the lint report told me what to go read next.
